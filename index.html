Ayurvedic Medicines

Ashwagandha

An adaptogenic herb known for its stress-relieving properties.

Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)

  • Properties: Adaptogenic, rejuvenative, and anti-stress.
  • Benefits:
    • Reduces cortisol levels, helping combat stress and anxiety.
    • Enhances stamina, strength, and energy levels.
    • Improves brain function, including memory and focus.
  • Common Uses:
    • Used in stress-relief tonics and immunity-boosting supplements.

Triphala

A traditional herbal formulation known for its digestive properties.

Triphala

  • Composition: Amalaki, Bibhitaki, Haritaki.
  • Properties: Laxative, detoxifying, and rejuvenative.
  • Benefits:
    • Improves digestion and prevents constipation.
    • Detoxifies the body and supports liver health.
    • Enhances immunity and promotes overall vitality.
  • Common Uses:
    • Used as a general health tonic and in digestive aids.

Turmeric

Known for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Turmeric (Curcuma longa)

  • Properties: Anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial.
  • Benefits:
    • Reduces inflammation in conditions like arthritis.
    • Improves skin health and wound healing.
  • Common Uses:
    • Key ingredient in blood-purifying formulas.

Shilajit

A mineral-rich resin known for its rejuvenative properties.

Shilajit

  • Source: A mineral-rich resin derived from rocks in the Himalayas.
  • Properties: Rejuvenative, adaptogenic, and anti-aging.
  • Benefits:
    • Boosts energy and stamina by enhancing mitochondrial function.
    • Helps with sexual health and improves fertility.
    • Contains fulvic acid, aiding nutrient absorption and detoxification.
  • Common Uses:
    • Used in vitality tonics and anti-aging supplements.

Tulsi

Immunomodulatory herb known for its respiratory-supportive properties.

Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum)

  • Properties: Immunomodulatory, antipyretic, and respiratory-supportive.
  • Benefits:
    • Enhances immune response to infections.
    • Reduces respiratory issues like asthma and bronchitis.
    • Acts as a natural remedy for fever and colds.
  • Common Uses:
    • Found in herbal teas, syrups for coughs, and immunity boosters.

Brahmi

Enhances memory, focus, and cognitive functions.

Brahmi (Bacopa monnieri)

  • Benefits: Enhances memory, focus, and cognitive functions.
  • Uses: Included in brain tonics and stress-relief formulations.

Neem

Purifies the blood and treats skin disorders.

Neem (Azadirachta indica)

  • Benefits: Purifies the blood, treats skin disorders, and combats infections.
  • Uses: Found in detox formulations and skincare products.

Guggul

Supports heart health and aids in weight management.

Guggul (Commiphora mukul)

  • Benefits: Lowers cholesterol, aids weight loss, and manages joint pain.
  • Uses: Used in heart health and weight management formulations.

Aloe Vera

Aids in digestion and promotes skin health.

Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis)

  • Benefits: Aids in digestion, promotes skin health, and boosts immunity.
  • Uses: Widely used in digestive tonics and cosmetic applications.

Yoga Asanas

Sukhasana (Easy Pose)

Calms the mind, improves focus, and gently opens hips.

Sukhasana (Easy Pose)

How to Do It: Sit cross-legged with a straight spine, hands resting on your knees, and close your eyes for 2-3 minutes. Focus on your breath.

Benefits: Calms the mind, improves focus, and gently opens hips.

Marjaryasana-Bitilasana (Cat-Cow Pose)

Warms up the spine, improves posture, and reduces back tension.

Marjaryasana-Bitilasana (Cat-Cow Pose)

How to Do It: Start in a tabletop position. Inhale as you arch your back (Cow), and exhale as you round your back (Cat). Repeat for 6-8 rounds.

Benefits: Warms up the spine, improves posture, and reduces back tension.

Tadasana (Mountain Pose)

Improves posture, enhances focus, and activates the whole body.

Tadasana (Mountain Pose)

How to Do It: Stand tall with feet together, arms by your side. Stretch your arms overhead and reach upward while keeping your feet grounded. Hold for 1 minute.

Benefits: Improves posture, enhances focus, and activates the whole body.

Surya Namaskar (Sun Salutation)

Boosts energy, stretches the entire body, and enhances cardiovascular health.

Surya Namaskar (Sun Salutation)

How to Do It: Perform 5-6 rounds of Sun Salutations, which involve a sequence of 12 poses (e.g., forward folds, lunges, plank, and upward-facing dog).

Benefits: Boosts energy, stretches the entire body, and enhances cardiovascular health.

Virabhadrasana II (Warrior II Pose)

Strengthens the legs, stretches hips, and improves stamina.

Virabhadrasana II (Warrior II Pose)

How to Do It: Step one foot back into a lunge, arms extended parallel to the floor, gaze over the front hand. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Benefits: Strengthens the legs, stretches hips, and improves stamina.

Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog)

Stretches the hamstrings, calves, and spine while calming the mind.

Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog)

How to Do It: From a tabletop position, lift your hips toward the ceiling to create an inverted V shape. Hold for 1 minute.

Benefits: Stretches the hamstrings, calves, and spine while calming the mind.

Utkatasana (Chair Pose)

Strengthens thighs, core, and lower back.

Utkatasana (Chair Pose)

How to Do It: Stand with feet together, bend your knees as if sitting in a chair, and raise your arms overhead. Hold for 30 seconds.

Benefits: Strengthens thighs, core, and lower back.

Vrksasana (Tree Pose)

Improves balance, focus, and strengthens leg muscles.

Vrksasana (Tree Pose)

How to Do It: Stand on one leg, place the sole of the opposite foot on your inner thigh or shin, and bring palms together in front of your chest. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Benefits: Improves balance, focus, and strengthens leg muscles.

Vasisthasana (Side Plank Pose)

Strengthens arms, core, and improves balance.

Vasisthasana (Side Plank Pose)

How to Do It: From a plank position, shift your weight to one hand and the edge of your foot, stacking your feet. Raise the other arm toward the sky. Hold for 30 seconds on each side.

Benefits: Strengthens arms, core, and improves balance.

Navasana (Boat Pose)

Strengthens the core and improves concentration.

Navasana (Boat Pose)

How to Do It: Sit on the floor, lift your legs, and balance on your sit bones while keeping the spine straight and arms parallel to the ground. Hold for 30 seconds.

Benefits: Strengthens the core and improves concentration.

Paschimottanasana (Seated Forward Bend)

Stretches the hamstrings, relieves stress, and calms the mind.

Paschimottanasana (Seated Forward Bend)

How to Do It: Sit with legs extended straight, reach forward to hold your feet or shins, and fold gently. Hold for 1 minute.

Benefits: Stretches the hamstrings, relieves stress, and calms the mind.

Setu Bandhasana (Bridge Pose)

Strengthens the lower back and opens the chest.

Setu Bandhasana (Bridge Pose)

How to Do It: Lie on your back, bend your knees, and lift your hips toward the ceiling while pressing your feet into the mat. Hold for 30 seconds.

Benefits: Strengthens the lower back and opens the chest.

Viparita Karani (Legs-Up-the-Wall Pose)

Relieves tired legs, improves circulation, and promotes relaxation.

Viparita Karani (Legs-Up-the-Wall Pose)

How to Do It: Lie on your back with your legs extended up against a wall. Hold for 3-5 minutes.

Benefits: Relieves tired legs, improves circulation, and promotes relaxation.

Savasana (Corpse Pose)

Deep relaxation and stress relief.

Savasana (Corpse Pose)

How to Do It: Lie flat on your back, arms by your side, palms facing upward. Close your eyes and relax your body completely. Hold for 5 minutes.

Benefits: Deep relaxation and stress relief.
